## Content

Local First Field Notes explores a simple idea: useful notes should not depend on a network connection or an account being available at the moment of capture.

The prototype keeps writing on the device first, makes sync state visible, and treats publishing as a separate editorial action. A note can remain private, move between devices, or become a small public document without changing the original record.

The visual system borrows from field journals: generous margins, quiet status labels, and a clear distinction between an observation, its revision history, and the version eventually shared.
